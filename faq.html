Answers to common questions about our appraisal services.

What is a real estate appraisal? ▼

A real estate appraisal is an independent, professional opinion of a property's market value. Appraisals are conducted by licensed or certified appraisers who analyze the property, local market conditions, and comparable sales to arrive at a supported value conclusion.

How long does an appraisal take? ▼

Most residential appraisals are completed within 5-10 business days from the date of the property inspection. Complex assignments, such as retrospective or estate appraisals, may require additional time. We provide an estimated timeline with every quote.

How much does an appraisal cost? ▼

Appraisal fees vary based on the property type, complexity, purpose, and scope of work. We provide detailed quotes before every assignment. Request a quote through our online form for a prompt response.

What types of appraisals do you offer? ▼

We offer private appraisals, divorce appraisals, estate appraisals, retrospective appraisals, new construction appraisals, FHA appraisals, green home appraisals, and mass appraisal consulting services.

What areas do you serve? ▼

We primarily serve the Triangle region of North Carolina, including Durham, Raleigh, Chapel Hill, Cary, Apex, and surrounding communities in Durham, Wake, Orange, and neighboring counties. Visit our Service Area page for the complete list of communities and counties we serve.

How do I request a quote? ▼

You can request a quote through our online form, call us directly, or email info@designerhomesre.com. We typically respond within one business day.

What is a retrospective appraisal? ▼

A retrospective appraisal determines the value of a property as of a past date. These are commonly needed for estate settlements (date-of-death valuations), tax appeals, litigation, and financial reporting.

Do you offer a client portal? ▼

Yes. Our secure client portal allows you to track your assignment status, receive updates, and download completed reports at your convenience. Learn more about our client portal.

What is mass appraisal consulting? ▼

Mass appraisal is the valuation of multiple properties using standardized methods and statistical analysis. We provide consulting services to government agencies, tax offices, and jurisdictions for reappraisal and assessment purposes. Learn more about mass appraisal consulting.

Are your appraisals accepted by courts and lenders? ▼

Yes. Our appraisals are prepared in compliance with the Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP) and are accepted by courts, lenders, attorneys, and government agencies.
